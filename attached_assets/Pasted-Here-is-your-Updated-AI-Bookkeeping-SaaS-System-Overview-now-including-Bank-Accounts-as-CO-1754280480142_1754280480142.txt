## ✅ **AI Bookkeeping SaaS System Overview (v2): Transaction Flow, Chart of Accounts, Categorization, and Bank Integration**

---

### 1. 🏦 **Bank Feed & Account Linking**

* **Source**: Transactions and account data are pulled via a bank feed (e.g., **Plaid**).
* **When user connects bank**:

  * All accounts (Chequing, Savings, Credit Cards, etc.) are imported.
  * Each is saved in the **Chart of Accounts** under the appropriate type:

    * **Assets** → Chequing, Savings, PayPal
    * **Liabilities** → Credit Cards, Lines of Credit
* These become selectable "bank accounts" in your app, and are essential for proper transaction mapping.

---

### 2. 📘 **Chart of Accounts (COA)**

* A centralized list of all accounts used for categorizing money flow.
* Every COA account includes:

  * `account_id`
  * `account_name` (e.g., "TD Chequing", "Meals & Entertainment")
  * `account_type`: Asset, Liability, Revenue, Expense, Equity
  * `tax_settings` (taxable, exempt, zero-rated)
  * `is_bank_account` (true/false)

**COA Structure Example:**

```plaintext
Assets
  - 1010: TD Chequing
  - 1020: PayPal
Liabilities
  - 2010: Visa Business Card
Revenue
  - 4000: Service Income
Expenses
  - 5000: Office Supplies
  - 5040: Meals & Entertainment
Equity
  - 3000: Owner Draw
```

---

### 3. 🔁 **Transaction Lifecycle**

| Step               | Description                                                                                                                                     |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1️⃣ Import         | Transactions are pulled from Plaid with amount, date, description, and originating account.                                                     |
| 2️⃣ Storage        | Stored in `transactions` table with references to both the **bank account (Asset/Liability)** and **categorization account (Revenue/Expense)**. |
| 3️⃣ Categorization | AI engine or rules engine suggests a category (e.g., “Software Subscriptions”).                                                                 |
| 4️⃣ Matching       | If a receipt exists, OCR parsing matches the receipt to a transaction.                                                                          |
| 5️⃣ Posting        | Once approved, it's recorded as a double-entry transaction.                                                                                     |

---

### 4. 🧮 **Double-Entry Posting Logic**

Example:

> User pays \$75 for lunch using TD Chequing

```plaintext
DR: Meals & Entertainment (Expense)
CR: TD Chequing (Asset)
```

Another example:

> Client pays \$1,000 deposited to PayPal

```plaintext
DR: PayPal (Asset)
CR: Service Income (Revenue)
```

---

### 5. 🧠 **AI-Powered Categorization Engine**

* Uses natural language processing + historical tagging + keyword rules.
* Looks at:

  * Merchant name
  * Description text
  * Amount pattern
  * Past categorizations by user
* Predicts best matching account from the COA.
* If confidence is high, auto-categorizes; if not, flags for review.

---

### 6. 🧾 **Receipt Upload & Matching**

* Users upload receipts.
* System uses **Mindee OCR** to extract:

  * Date
  * Amount
  * Vendor
* Auto-matches to transactions by date/amount proximity and merchant.
* Matched receipts are linked and viewable.

---

### 7. 📊 **Reporting & Real-Time Sync**

As transactions are approved:

* ✅ Income Statement updates Revenue - Expenses
* ✅ Balance Sheet updates Asset, Liability, and Equity accounts
* ✅ GST/HST Summary updates based on taxable categories
* ✅ Expense Breakdown shows category totals (with receipts if matched)

---

### 8. 🧩 **User Interaction Flow**

* User sees:

  * **New Transactions** → Suggested categories
  * **Receipts** → Upload and auto-match
  * **Reports** → Updated live as transactions are categorized
* Can override AI suggestions or recategorize any transaction
